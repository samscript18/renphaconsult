Top 10 Exciting Scholoarship for International Students

Are you planning to study abroad but sceptical about the ways you can fund it?
Studying abroad is a dream for many, but keeping in mind the expenses and exhaustive process of admission, students tend to drop the idea, but you don’t have to!

Do you know that most of the top study destinations offer convenient ways to fund and support your education abroad? One way to do so is by applying for a well-funded scholarship. Now you may ask, “How do I apply for a scholarship? What are the top scholarships for international students? What are the eligibility criteria?” We have the answer for you!

Top 10 Scholarships for International Students to Study Abroad

There are numerous scholarships for students seeking admission across the world. These scholarships can be offered either by the university or by some external sources.

Here are the top 10 scholarships for International students to study abroad:

#1. Chevening Scholarship (UK)

Offered to international students seeking a one-year master's degree from UK universities. The Chevening scholarship is available for all international students coming from Chevening-eligible countries. However, students must have an admission offer letter to apply for the scholarship. Also, applicants must have at least 2 years of work experience to be eligible and satisfy the language proficiency requirements. Although language proficiency is now optional, adding it can increase your chances.

Benefits: fully funded, including tuition fees, living, allowances, etc.

#2. Fulbright Scholarships (USA)

Fullbright is one of the most popular scholarships among international students who want to study in the USA. Offered to over 4,000 students from 155 different countries, only a selected meritorious student can avail of all the benefits. It is a government-based scholarship eligible for all graduates, teachers, artists, and professionals, provided they have studied for at least one year in the USA. The eligibility for the scholarship may vary with the country.

Benefits: 8000+ grants covering tuition fees, living expenses, travel expenses, etc.

#3. Endeavour Postgraduate Awards (Australia)

The Endeavour postgraduate awards are the Australian government's internationally competitive, merit-based scholarships for eligible non-Australians to study postgraduate programs at a Master's or PhD level. Your course level can be coursework or research in any field for more than 4 years. You must be a permanent resident of a participating country to be eligible for the scholarship. High-level academics with good academic profiles are the selection criteria for it.

Benefits: Up to 272,500 AUD including stipend, allowances, etc.

#3. Endeavour Postgraduate Awards (Australia)

The Endeavour postgraduate awards are the Australian government's internationally competitive, merit-based scholarships for eligible non-Australians to study postgraduate programs at a Master's or PhD level. Your course level can be coursework or research in any field for more than 4 years. You must be a permanent resident of a participating country to be eligible for the scholarship. High-level academics with good academic profiles are the selection criteria for it.

Benefits: Up to 272,500 AUD including stipend, allowances, etc.

#4. Eiffel Excellence Scholarship Programme (France)

Established by the French Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs attracts international students from across the world to pursue their master's and PhD programs in France. Applicants must be enrolled in engineering or a joint doctoral degree program, including several fields of study. The scholarship does not cover any tuition fees; instead, it is available under various secondary costs, from your local travelling fees to monthly allowances.

Benefits: Euro 1,181(INR 1,06,000 to Euro 1,700 (INR 1,53,000) per month.

#5. GREAT Scholarships (UK)

The GREAT scholarship is offered to international students from 14 different countries for a wide range of 1 year taught postgraduate degree courses. During the 2023 -24 academic year, over 200 scholarships are provided by more than 49 universities across England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland. The UK's government jointly funds each scholarship with their participating institutions like UCL, University of Nottingham, New Castle University, etc.

Benefits: Euro 10,000 (INR 9,04,000) tuition fees.

#6. Rotary Foundation Global Study Grants (Global)

The Rotary Foundation offers scholarship funds for graduate-level students and research students for one to four years of study. This is available for any approved university where a Rotary club or district is located, granting tuition fees, transportation costs, living expenses, etc. The field of study can be in any one of the areas, such as disease prevention and treatment, peace and conflict prevention or resolution, water and sanitation, maternal and child health, basic education and literacy, and economic and community development.

Benefits: Up to 30,000 with other expenses.

#7. Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation Scholarships (Canada)

Pierre Foundation scholarships welcome applications from applicants from a wide range of disciplines in humanities and social sciences and business schools like public health and law. Applicants must be PhD students whose research must be related to one or more of the foundation's four central themes, such as people and their natural environment, human rights and dignity, responsible citizenship, Canada and the world.

Benefits: Up to 40,000 CAD stipend with up to 20,000 CAD research and travel allowances for three years.

#8. Commonwealth Scholarship

Commonwealth scholarship is offered to Masters and PhD students seeking admission to UK universities. This is distributed through the British Council to deserving students from Commonwealth partner countries. The applicants must have good proficiency in the English language and have secured at least 70% or more in their academics. Engineering, Agriculture, Social Sciences and Humanities, Pure and Applied Sciences students are eligible for the scholarship.

Benefits: Tuition Fees, living and travel expenses.

#9. Aga Khan Foundation International Scholarship Programme

Aga Khan scholarships are accepted in every university across the world except Germany, the UK, Austria, Sweden, Denmark, Netherlands, Italy, Ireland and Norway. The Aga Khan Foundation provides only a limited number of scholarships yearly for postgraduate students from their countries to develop effective leaders and scholars and prepare them for employment.

Benefits: Tuition Fees and living expenses.

#10. Joint Japan World Bank Graduate Scholarship Program (USA, Africa, and Japan)

This scholarship is awarded to all individuals from developing countries with professional experience and applying to seek admission to a master's degree in a development-related field. They were provided to students pursuing education in USA, Africa, Japan, Europe and Oceania universities. You must have at least three years of development experience and a bachelor's degree to qualify for the scholarship.

Benefits: Tuition Fees, monthly stipend, health insurance, travel allowances, etc.
